By leveraging real-time data, AI, and intelligent analytics, we enable teams to manage personnel safety, equipment, and environmental factors effectively, all while improving operational efficiency and reducing accidents.

3.5 CCTV
3.5.1 High definition pan/ tilt camera that can observe up to 1 KM in radius in an open environment (site).
3.5.2 Cameras must be installed at the highest point in an open area. The minimum height position is 2.5m from the floor.
3.5.3 Cameras positioned to monitor high risk areas (eg working at height, pipe racks and confined space entry points) and must be positioned at a maximum spacing of 75m.
3.5.3.1 Within the pipe rack areas, cameras must be fitted on each level to ensure adequate coverage as the work progresses and must provide the maximum viewing angle.
3.5.4 Cameras positioned on entry/ exit points.
3.5.5 Fixed cameras on roads for traffic monitoring and control.
3.5.6 Cameras are to be fully portable and powered by solar energy where possible.
3.5.7 Portable cameras (stationary or vehicle fixed) to be moved along with work activities (pipelines, well tie-ins etc.).
3.5.7.1 Camera positions to be monitored and reviewed on a monthly (minimum) basis to ensure they cover all active project site locations.
3.5.8 All Cameras to be Al enabled to detect unsafe acts and conditions.

4. Safety Control Centre (SCC)
 a  Workforce data: age, occupation, assigned location, nationality, site access approval, medical condition, safety training records.
 b  Work permit data: Location, authorized workers and equipment associated with active work permits. Supporting documentation relevant to work permits such as JSAS, Lift Plans, SIMOPS Plans and Emergency Response Plans to be accessible or referenced via SCC platforms.
 c  Buses + vehicles: Location, traffic control, driver's data, maintenance records.
 d  Cranes and Heavy Equipment: location, operator certification, inspection records, maintenance records and PWAS.
 e  Meteorological data: wind speed, temperature, heat index and high/low tide.
 f  Hazard Identification: high-risk activities based on active work on-site identified by the Work Permits and forecast by the two weeks lookahead, including mitigation required to control hazards.
 g  Surveillance System: high-resolution AI-enabled cameras provide real-time and recorded footage of work activities across the project site and identify unsafe acts and unsafe conditions.

4.1 Security
4.1.1 Access to the SCC room must be restricted at all times.
4.1.2 The SCC assigned room must be secured from the rest of the building by the use of a suitable and functional locked door.
4.1.3 Access to the room is restricted to approved personnel only as determined by the Project Team - Individuals who have access the SCC must have the required Saudi Aramco or Project ID.
4.1.4 No photos or recording are permitted within the SCC unless permitted by the Project Manager.

4.2 SCC Monitoring
4.2.1 The SCC must be manned at all times with a minimum of two (2) approved operators when personnel are working on site to ensure full coverage.
4.2.2 Only approved and trained personnel can be assigned to the SCC as per the requirements set out in section 5.5-SCC Operator.
4.2.3 Hardcopy of emergency contacts to be maintained and up to date within the SCC.
4.2.3.1 Emergency contacts to include contact details for Director, PM, SPE, PE, and all site HSE.

4.3 Notification Protocol
4.3.1 Any Notification/Alarms that is noted in the SCC must be addressed immediately
4.3.1.1 All notifications will be investigated by the assigned SCC operator, or delegates as assigned by the Project Manager.
4.3.1.2 All notifications observed as true (unsafe act/violation) will be reported to the site team immediately.
4.3.1.3 The SCC must be fitted with adequate means of communication so that the SCC Operator can contact the site team immediately as required.
4.3.1.4 Notification that are deemed as false will be reviewed and investigated if required.
4.3.2 All alarms and notification events must be documented in the SCC dally log book by the assigned personnel, true and faise alarms must both be logged and distinguished in the logbook.
4.3.2.1 Daily Alerts must be reported and reviewed by the Project Manager or his delegate on a daily bass.
4.3.2.2 Hardcopy of alerts to be mantained in the SCC and Manager to review and close alerts on a weekly basis.
4.3.2.3 The daily logbook must be issued to the department HSE team for further review and analysis at the end of each week.

4.4 SCC. Reporting and Analysis
4.4.1 The SCC log book will be issued to the department HSE team and department manager on a weekly basis.
4.4.2 The department HSE team will conduct a weekly review and analysis of the SCC logbook entries and identify trends such as high violation areas on the site.
4.4.3 All notifications observed as true (unsafe act/violation) will be reported to the site team immediately.
4.4.4 The SCC must be fitted with adequate means of communication so that the SCC Operator can contact the site team immediately as required.
4.4.5 A weekly report will be issued to the Project Manager from the SCC showing the following information as a minimum.
Daily Safety Observations
Any HSE related accidents and incidents
Identification of Life Saving Rules violations iand actions taken (eg. working without a permit
Identification of positive behaviors/actions
Weather Conditions
Site Manpower
Total amount of Vehicles being monitored
Vehicle violations and action taken
ENV Gas Monitoring
ENV CO2 monitoring
ENV Dust monitoring and management

4.5 SCC Formal Procedures
4.5.1 The SCC will have written and easy to understand procedures in place
4.5.2 The procedure must be readily available to the SCC operator and easily accessible

5.5 SCC Operator (Contractor)
5.5.1 Responsible to ensure that they are trained in the use of the technology and interface in the SCC.
5.5.2 SCC Operator must receive formal Safety Training, including a 24hr (min) OSHA, NEBOSH, NSC or other SAPO approved equivalent safety training program.
5.5.3 Responsible to monitor the system
5.5.4 Establish a process/ procedure for the operation of the SCC
5.5.5 Report any issues including IT issues and complete the daily log
